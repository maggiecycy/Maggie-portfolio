# 08 · Emotional Games and Self-Protection — Happy Women's Day!

> In dating, the most important thing for a girl to learn is not how to please, but how to filter.

Happy Women's Day! Today, I want to share some thoughts on dating and emotional games.

I used to think that associating romance with words like "games," "costs," and "transactions" seemed too cold and calculating. When I met someone who made my heart flutter, my first instinct was always to show my cards unreservedly: replying to messages instantly, actively finding topics to talk about, and making all sorts of excuses for their withdrawal. I thought that as long as I was sincere enough, I could exchange it for the same level of stability. That was until I felt extreme exhaustion amidst endless internal friction, anxiety, and their hot-and-cold behavior.

In modern dating culture, being someone who yearns for intimacy is just too tiring. Intimate relationships, at some unknown point, have turned into a game of human nature, bottom lines, and costs.

---

## Puncturing the Illusion of a "Two-Way Street"

It is too easy for us to mistake "low-cost probing" for a "mutual effort." Often, he just flirts with you a bit on WeChat, occasionally providing some sweet words and emotional value, and you've already played out a whole movie in your head. You think this is a mutual spark, but in reality, "he only dares to be slow because he's not afraid of losing you." A true mutual effort means you give a response, and he takes action; you give time, and he gives investment.

If a person is unwilling to advance even the most basic stable meetups in the early stages and constantly brainwashes you with "I'm busy with work," then this is not being slow to warm up; this is waiting and seeing—seeing if there is a better option, seeing if he can maintain your enthusiasm at the lowest possible cost. He only dares to be slow because he is not afraid of losing you.

At this stage, stopping the self-persuasion is the first step we must learn in self-protection. Both time and money are tests of whether he is willing to bear the cost of this relationship. If there are only sweet words and occasional romance without stable investment, then this relationship is destined to have no possibility of moving forward.

---

## What Comes Easy is Destined Not to Be Cherished

This is a reality that many girls (especially those with an anxious attachment style) are most reluctant to admit: the easier you are to get, the less anxious he is. A mechanism of conquest hides within male nature; things obtained too quickly are destined not to be cherished. When you are always on call, offering up your emotions, body, and future entirely, the other party loses any cost of loss. Never try to trade your body for a sense of certainty in a relationship, because once you give in, what he gets isn't love, but a daily reward categorized as "safe but boring."

There is a deep-seated flaw in human nature: without cost, there is no appreciation. If we don't set up hurdles and demand investment from the other person (time, energy, emotions, and even money), we will never be able to filter out the one who is truly willing to take risks for you. Only those who just want a short-term fling will rush you frantically.

Maintain a bit of an aloof aura, and don't deliberately force topics. This isn't being cold; it's using physical distance to test the other person's sincerity.

---

## From "Being Chosen" to "Actively Filtering"

In dating, the most dangerous psychological cue is "objectifying" yourself—constantly wondering what you look like in his eyes, constantly guessing whether his cold violence is because you did something wrong. In fact, cold violence is merely a character flaw in how a person handles conflict; it has nothing to do with whether he loves you or whether you did something wrong.

One way to take back the initiative is to establish a filtering mindset. When we go out on a date, we shouldn't treat the man as an audience member observing you. We are not going to an interview for a girlfriend position; we are there to examine whether the person sitting across from us meets the standards to enter our lives. Is he responsible? Can he provide stable emotions? Is he willing to bear the definition of a relationship?

If you find that you are only experiencing one-sided depletion, cut off contact promptly. Even if your brain is in agony, short-term pain is better than long-term suffering; even if you can only sleep heavily for two days, you must wake up and immediately get back to living your own life. Because your life doesn't need to be saved by anyone.

---

## IV. The Logical Loopholes of the Defense System

There is a massive amount of "advanced guides" on the internet regarding dating and emotions. They teach us not to have heart-to-hearts, share our vulnerabilities, or talk about our family of origin with someone we just met. They teach us not to date exclusively, but to meet multiple people to break the obsession. However, if we follow them completely, a huge logical loophole emerges: **we are using an "avoidant" coldness to forcibly mask our "anxious" instincts.**

Going on dates with the goal of "having some mindless fun" or "having high energy and wanting to socialize" can indeed protect us from getting hurt, but it also completely strips away our ability to build deep connections. When we don't even dare to expose a sliver of genuine vulnerability, how can we filter out the person who truly resonates with our soul?

---

## V. Reclaiming Energy Inward is the Ultimate Filter

The complexity of society lies in human nature, and the best places to understand human nature are the arenas of vanity and romance. The dating scene, much like the workplace, is an excellent teacher for us to ponder the underlying logic of the world and improve ourselves.

A truly secure partner is someone who enjoys being with you but is also comfortable being alone—no thrilling drama, just warmth and groundedness. But before meeting someone like that, we must first learn to be our own safety net. We should lower our fantasies (don't over-anticipate), but we must never lower our standards (don't accept half-heartedness). Even if a relationship ends in no contact, and even if your brain aches, you must keep your body from going numb. Even if you sleep the days away occasionally, you must get right back up to make money, read books, and work.

Please be confident at all times that you can live perfectly well on your own, rather than begging someone else to save your impoverished life.

I still look forward to love, and I am willing to love boldly. But I no longer live on fantasies, nor will I allow anyone to keep me hanging on through procrastination.

**See you around.**
