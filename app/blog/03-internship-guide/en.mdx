

# 03 · Big Tech Internship Application Guide

> After weeks of intense searching, constant resume iterations and filtering through a sea of online info, I’ve compiled this guide to help CS stu bridge the information gap.

---

## 1. Channel Strategy: Finding the Hidden 90%

* **Successor Recruitment**: The ultimate shortcut. Departing interns often find their own replacements, bypassing HR filters to reach the Mentor's desk directly. Selection focuses on "immediate readiness."
* **Information Sources**: Focus on XiaoHongShu, specialized internship apps (like Internship-Seng), campus communities, and alumni networks.
* **Hidden Portals**: Applications via official WeChat accounts or company portals have much higher view rates than overcrowded platforms like LinkedIn or Boss Zhipin.
* **Misplaced Competition**: Avoid the "Pure Developer" Red Ocean. Target Product Assistant, Tech Support, QA, Ops AI, or Project Management—roles that value a CS background but are less saturated.
* **Foreign Firms**: Students with average backgrounds should eye local offices of European or American firms; they offer better balance and are more inclusive of beginners.
* **Efficiency Priority**: Referrals (Successors) > Referral Links > Official Portals > Job Apps.

---

## 2. Resume Building: From "Logbook" to "Tech Spec"

**HR Screening Hierarchy:**
1. **Relevant Experience**: Vertical alignment with the role beats prestige schools with zero experience.
2. **Educational Background**: Top-tier universities (though successors are often hired from a broader pool).
3. **Skill Stack**: Highlight Excel (Vlookup/Pivot Tables), Python, SQL, and CS Fundamentals (OS/Network/DB).
4. **Soft Skills**: Communication, logical thinking, and stress tolerance.

**Project Engineering:**
Follow the **Problem → Action → Result** logic. Package course projects or capstones as "Production-Ready Projects."
* *Example*: Instead of "Assisted in development," use "Optimized XX management system, resolved critical latency issues, and improved performance by 10%."

**Endorsements:**
Place your GitHub contribution graph or technical blog link prominently. Use AI tools to polish your resume, but ensure you can defend every bullet point during the interview.

---

## 3. Application Tactics: The 10-Second Muscle Memory

**Naming Convention:**
Always use **PDF format**.
`Name + University + Major + Grad Year + Duration + Start Date + Days/Week`

**The 3-Paragraph Cover Letter:**
1. **Basic Info**: University, major, and availability.
2. **Core Strengths**: 3 tags/projects that perfectly match the JD.
3. **Motivation**: Briefly express your research on the role and your desire to join.

**Volume is Key:**
Apply to 10 roles daily as a habit; during "Aggressive Mode," push for **30–50 applications per day**. Verify company email suffixes (e.g., tencent.com) to avoid scams.

---

## 4. Interview Strategy: Beyond Robotic Responses

* **The Golden 3 Minutes**: Introduction Formula: Who I am + 3 most relevant achievements + Why I want this role.
* **Human-to-Human Mode**: Don't read from a script. Maintain eye contact and tailor your answers to the JD's keywords.
* **Logic Bridging**: If you hit a technical blind spot, explain your troubleshooting logic (Server → Network → Database) to demonstrate your problem-solving process.
* **Honesty with Ambition**: If you don't know an answer, admit it, then explain how you've handled similar concepts and your willingness to learn.
* **The "Cheat Sheet"**: For online interviews, stick sticky notes near your camera with keywords for your intro and project logic to prevent blanking out.

---

## 5. Offer Negotiation: Survival of the Bold

* **The Leverage Mindset**: CS job hunting is "Survival of the Bold." Secure an offer first to gain the upper hand.
* **The "A vs B" Tactic**: *"I have another offer expiring soon, but this company is my top choice. Could I check on the feedback status?"*
* **Availability Hack**: When asked about duration, always commit to: Immediate start, 5 days a week, 6 months duration, and a desire for full-time conversion.

---

## 6. Red Flags & The 14-Day Action Plan

**Red Flags:**
* **Dirty Work Identification**: JDs frequenting words like "Filtering, Auditing, Cleaning, Copying" usually signal cheap labor roles.
* **Training Scams**: Blacklist any "internship" that requires upfront fees or unpaid training.
* **Salary Benchmark**: In Tier-1 cities, CS internships typically pay **200–400 RMB/day**.

**14-Day Aggressive Plan:**
* **Day 1–2**: Resume refinement, target 2–3 specific roles, and master your elevator pitch.
* **Day 3–10**: Submit 30–50 applications daily (40% Dream, 40% Match, 20% Safety).
* **Day 11–14**: Debrief within 1 hour after every interview.

---

## 7. The Reverse Interview Checklist

Confirm these details before signing to ensure the internship's value:
1. **Work Content**: Does it involve core project output?
2. **Mentorship**: Will there be a dedicated Mentor?
3. **Conversion**: What are the requirements for a full-time return offer?
4. **Compliance**: Will there be a formal internship agreement and certificate?
5. **Perks**: Salary, meal allowances, and housing subsidies.

---

### Core Mottoes

* **Information is Wealth**: Execution trumps talent every time.
* **Goal-Oriented Mindset**: From Day 1, think about what quantifiable results you can add to your resume.
* **Peak Window**: Post-Chinese New Year (February onwards) is the golden window for demand.
* **The Bold Gain Choice**: Your first internship is a springboard; be brave enough to negotiate.
